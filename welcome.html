Ultrastructure & Segments

🧬 Glomerulus

Fenestrated capillaries under high pressure (55-60 mmHg). Acts as selective filter — retains proteins & cells.

🥣 Bowman's Capsule

Double-walled epithelial cup; collects ultrafiltrate. Podocyte foot processes form filtration slits.

🌀 Proximal Tubule

Reabsorbs 65% Na⁺, 100% glucose & amino acids via brush border. Major solute recovery site.

📏 Loop of Henle

Countercurrent multiplier creates medullary gradient; crucial for water conservation.

⚡ Distal Tubule

Hormonal regulation (aldosterone, PTH). Fine-tuning of Ca²⁺, Na⁺, pH balance.

🌊 Collecting Duct

Final osmoregulation under ADH control; concentrates urine up to 1200 mOsm/L.

Dynamic Physiology: 3 Core Processes

💧 Filtration

Hydrostatic pressure drives fluid into Bowman's space. GFR ~125 mL/min, regulated by autoregulation.

🔄 Reabsorption

Active transport reclaims water, glucose, ions. Na⁺/K⁺ ATPase powers 99% efficiency.

🧪 Secretion

Adds H⁺, K⁺, drugs into tubule lumen; essential for acid-base homeostasis and toxin removal.

Advanced Insight: The countercurrent multiplier generates up to 1200 mOsm/kg osmotic gradient — enabling mammals to produce hypertonic urine and survive arid environments.
